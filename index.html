About

Zehra is a DPhil student in the Department of Pharmacology at the University of Oxford. Her research explores how peripheral and central inflammatory pathways contribute to neuroinflammation and behavioural change after traumatic brain injury, using rodent models and molecular intervention strategies.

She completed an MSc in Integrated Immunology at Oxford, where she developed a strong foundation in molecular and cellular immunology, as well as bioinformatics and experimental design. Before that, she studied Biomedical Sciences at Queen Mary University of London, graduating with first-class honours.

Across roles at the William Harvey Research Institute, Imperial College London, UCL, and Oxford, Zehra has worked on projects related to inflammatory disease mechanisms, cytokine-driven signalling, and cell-based models of pathology. She aims to build a research career that integrates mechanistic neuroscience with translational and patient-centred perspectives.

Research

Current DPhil project

Zehra’s doctoral work examines how inflammatory mediators arising from peripheral tissues and the central nervous system shape neuroinflammatory responses and behavioural outcomes following traumatic brain injury. Her project uses in vivo models to investigate specific pathways that may influence both acute and longer-term changes in brain function.

She is developing experience with RNA- and viral-vector–based tools, cytokine profiling, and behavioural assays, with the longer-term aim of informing strategies that could be relevant for future therapeutic approaches.
